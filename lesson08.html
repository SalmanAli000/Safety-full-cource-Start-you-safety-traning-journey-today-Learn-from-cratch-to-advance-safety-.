Lesson 8: Lockout-Tagout (LOTO)

Overview

Control hazardous energy by isolation, tagging and permits.

Key Topics

  • Energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic)
  • LOTO devices
  • Permit-to-work
  • Verification

Detailed Content

LOTO prevents unexpected energisation. Identify all energy sources, isolate them, apply lock(s) and tag(s), and verify zero energy before work. Only the person who applied the lock removes it, unless authorised procedures exist.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Isolation & Verification

List all energy sources, use LOTO, verify zero energy, and test before work. Record every isolation.

Example / Case

A machine is locked out and a checklist is completed before guards are removed for repair.

Checklist

  • Identify all energy sources
  • Use durable locks and tags
  • Verify isolation before work begins
