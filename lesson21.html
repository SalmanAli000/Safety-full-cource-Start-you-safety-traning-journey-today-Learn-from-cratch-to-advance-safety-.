Lesson 21: First Aid Basics

Overview

Basic first aid principles: primary survey, CPR, bleeding control and when to call emergency services.

Key Topics

  • Primary survey (DRABC)
  • CPR basics
  • Controlling severe bleeding
  • Shock recognition
  • AED use

Detailed Content

Use DRABC (Danger, Response, Airway, Breathing, Circulation) to assess casualties. For cardiac arrest, begin CPR and use an AED if available. Control severe bleeding with direct pressure and tourniquets when appropriate and treat for shock by keeping the person warm and reassured.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Medical & Response Details

Establish communication with local EMS, maintain casualty care records, and rehearse shock/trauma scenarios.

Example / Case

A workplace trains staff in CPR and places an AED near the reception area.

Checklist

  • Train first-aiders
  • Keep first-aid kits stocked
  • Call emergency services early when needed
