Lesson 22: Emergency Response & Reporting

Overview

How to respond to incidents, report them and learn from root cause analysis.

Key Topics

  • Immediate response
  • Incident reporting
  • Root cause analysis
  • Corrective actions
  • Records

Detailed Content

Respond to protect life and prevent escalation, then preserve evidence for investigation. Report incidents promptly using forms, investigate to find root causes and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Investigation & CAPA

Use root cause tools (5 Whys, Fishbone), prioritise corrective actions, assign owners and verify effectiveness.

Example / Case

After a forklift collision, the site stops operations, secures scene, reports the incident and performs an investigation.

Checklist

  • Prioritise life and safety
  • Report all incidents and near-misses
  • Implement corrective measures
