Lesson 17: Machine & Equipment Safety

Overview

Guards, safe start/stop procedures and maintenance to prevent machine injuries.

Key Topics

  • Fixed and interlocked guards
  • Start/stop controls
  • Permits for maintenance
  • Lock-off procedures
  • Inspections

Detailed Content

Machines must have guards to prevent contact with moving parts. Interlocks that stop machines when guards open are effective. Use permits and isolate energy before maintenance; perform pre-start checks after repairs.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Safe Maintenance

Planned maintenance, isolation, test after maintenance, spares management, and guarding integrity verification.

Example / Case

A press has an interlocked guard and two-hand control to ensure safe operation.

Checklist

  • Never bypass guards
  • Use proper isolation for maintenance
  • Train operators on safe use
