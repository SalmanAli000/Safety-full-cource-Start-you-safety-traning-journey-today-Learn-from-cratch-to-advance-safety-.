Lesson 2: Types of Hazards

Overview

Identify physical, chemical, biological, ergonomic and psychosocial hazards.

Key Topics

  • Physical hazards (noise, heat)
  • Chemical hazards (acids, solvents)
  • Biological hazards
  • Ergonomic risks
  • Psychosocial factors

Detailed Content

Each hazard type requires different controls: engineering (guards, ventilation), administrative (procedures, rotation) and PPE. Hazard identification uses walk-throughs, checklists and MSDS (SDS) for chemicals.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Example / Case

A warehouse with manual lifting uses trolleys and team-lift procedures after repeated back injuries.

Checklist

  • List hazards before a job
  • Use SDS for chemicals
  • Apply controls in order (hierarchy of controls)
