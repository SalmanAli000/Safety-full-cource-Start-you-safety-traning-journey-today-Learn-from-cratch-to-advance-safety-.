Lesson 5: Classes of Fire & Extinguishers

Overview

Fire classes (A,B,C,D,F) and selecting correct extinguishers and usage.

Key Topics

  • Class A (combustibles)
  • Class B (liquids)
  • Class C (gases/electrical)
  • Class D (metals)
  • Class F/K (cooking oils)

Detailed Content

Select an extinguisher matching fire class. Water for Class A; foam or dry chemical for Class B; CO2 or dry powder for electrical; specialist powders for metal fires. Never use water on oil or electrical fires.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Example / Case

A kitchen installs a Class K extinguisher and staff are trained to isolate fuel and never pour water on grease fires.

Checklist

  • Know extinguisher types
  • Inspect extinguishers monthly
  • Train staff in PASS technique (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
