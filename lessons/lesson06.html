Lesson 6: Fire Evacuation & Drills

Overview

Planning evacuation routes, assembly points and regular drills.

Key Topics

  • Evacuation plan
  • Roles (wardens, floor marshals)
  • Assembly points
  • Drill frequency
  • Accountability

Detailed Content

Evacuation planning maps routes, ensures exits are clear and assigns roles. Drills test timings, communication and identify problems. Ensure accessibility for all and account for visitors and contractors.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Permits & Rescue

Always use permit-to-work where required. Prepare rescue plans, equipment and trained rescuers before high-risk tasks.

Example / Case

An office conducts quarterly drills and updates the plan after a drill showed blocked exit signs.

Checklist

  • Maintain clear exits
  • Assign and train fire wardens
  • Practice realistic drills regularly

Short Quiz

  1. How often should drills occur?
  2. Who checks that everyone is out?
  3. What to do if an exit is blocked during a drill?

Answers at bottom.

Conclusion

Drills build muscle memory; review and improve after each drill.
