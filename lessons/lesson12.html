Lesson 12: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — Head, Eye, Face

Overview

Selection, inspection and correct use of helmets, goggles and face shields.

Key Topics

  • Helmet types and standards
  • Safety goggles vs glasses
  • Face shields for splash/impact
  • Maintenance

Detailed Content

Choose PPE based on hazard assessment. Helmets protect against impact and penetration; goggles form a seal for dust or splashes; face shields protect the whole face in addition to goggles. Inspect before use and store correctly.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Example / Case

Construction workers wear helmets and goggles during demolition and replace damaged PPE immediately.

Checklist

  • Select PPE to match hazard
  • Inspect and replace damaged PPE
  • Train in correct fit and use
