Lesson 10: Chemical Storage & Segregation

Overview

Storing corrosives, flammables and toxics safely with segregation and bunding.

Key Topics

  • Flammable cabinets
  • Corrosive segregation
  • Bunding and spill trays
  • Ventilation
  • Labeling

Detailed Content

Store chemicals by compatibility groups. Use approved cabinets for flammables, corrosives in trays and keep quantities controlled. Ensure good ventilation and secondary containment to limit spread during spills.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Chemical Handling Deep Dive

Include storage capacities, segregation matrices, emergency neutralisation procedures, inhalation limits, and waste disposal routes. Ensure local ventilation and spill containment plans.

Example / Case

A workshop keeps small volumes of solvents in a ventilated flammable cabinet with spill trays.

Checklist

  • Segregate incompatible chemicals
  • Limit quantities
  • Label storage areas
