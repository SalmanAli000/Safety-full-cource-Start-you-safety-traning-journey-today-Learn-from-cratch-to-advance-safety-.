Lesson 20: Working at Heights

Overview

Controls for working at height: planning, fall prevention, PPE and rescue.

Key Topics

  • Risk assessment and planning
  • Edge protection and guardrails
  • Use of harnesses and lanyards
  • Scaffold and ladder safety
  • Rescue plans

Detailed Content

Plan work at height, use collective protection like guardrails where possible, provide training and ensure fall arrest systems are inspected. Have rescue plans because fall-arrest may leave a suspended worker at risk.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Permits & Rescue

Always use permit-to-work where required. Prepare rescue plans, equipment and trained rescuers before high-risk tasks.

Equipment Selection & Inspection

Inspect harnesses, connectors, anchor points; calculate fall clearance; use certified PPE and regularly inspect per standards.

Example / Case

A roof team uses edge protection and a harness with a rescue plan before starting work.

Checklist

  • Use guardrails first
  • Inspect harnesses and anchors
  • Train for ladder safety
