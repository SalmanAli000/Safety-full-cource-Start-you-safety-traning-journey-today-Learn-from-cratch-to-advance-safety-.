Lesson 1: Introduction to Workplace Safety

Overview

Basics of workplace safety, its importance, and core principles.

Key Topics

  • Definition and purpose
  • Common hazards
  • Worker and employer responsibilities
  • Basic legal principles

Detailed Content

Workplace safety means creating and maintaining an environment where risks are identified, assessed and controlled to prevent injury and illness. The three core principles are: anticipation, recognition, and control of hazards. A safety culture encourages reporting, continuous learning, and shared responsibility.

Employers must provide safe systems of work and training; workers must follow procedures and use PPE.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Training & Competence

Establish baseline training, induction, toolbox talks, and competency assessment for new workers and contractors.

Example / Case

A factory introduces a permit-to-work system after a near-miss to prevent recurrence.

Checklist

  • Identify risks during task planning
  • Provide task-specific training
  • Use appropriate PPE
  • Follow permit and isolation procedures
