Lesson 23: Safety Culture & Continuous Improvement

Overview

Building a positive safety culture, leadership, training and continuous improvement.

Key Topics

  • Leadership commitment
  • Worker involvement
  • Training and competence
  • Near-miss reporting
  • Audits and reviews

Detailed Content

Safety culture depends on leadership, visible commitment, open reporting and learning. Encourage reporting of near-misses, conduct audits, review performance and involve workers in safety decisions.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

KPIs & Continuous Improvement

Set leading and lagging indicators, perform audits, and share lessons learned across teams.

Example / Case

Management holds regular toolbox talks, acts on suggestions and recognises safe behaviours.

Checklist

  • Leaders must lead by example
  • Encourage reporting
  • Use audits to improve
