Lesson 15: Body Protection

Overview

Coveralls, aprons and flame-resistant clothing for various hazards.

Key Topics

  • Coverall types
  • Flame-resistant (FR) clothing
  • High-visibility garments
  • Contamination control

Detailed Content

Body protection reduces injury from heat, chemicals, and contamination. FR clothing prevents clothing ignition. High-visibility garments reduce traffic collisions. Change contaminated clothing safely and dispose or launder per procedures.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Example / Case

A petrol station requires staff to wear FR clothing and high-visibility jackets during night shifts.

Checklist

  • Select clothing per hazard
  • Use FR where fire risk exists
  • Launder contaminated clothing separately
