Lesson 14: Hand & Foot Protection

Overview

Gloves and safety footwear selection for cut, chemical and impact protection.

Key Topics

  • Material selection for gloves
  • Cut-resistant gloves
  • Chemical-resistant gloves
  • Safety boots standards
  • Inspection

Detailed Content

Select gloves by hazard: nitrile for many chemicals, leather for mechanical work, cut-resistant for sharp edges. Safety boots with toe protection, puncture-resistant soles and slip-resistant outsoles prevent many injuries.

Beginner Level

Foundational knowledge: definitions, simple examples, and immediate safe actions.

  • Understand key terms and hazards.
  • Recognise common warning signs and PPE.
  • Follow simple safe work steps and reporting.

Intermediate Level

Procedures, permits, tools and supervision: practical application and routine controls.

  • Implement standard operating procedures and checklists.
  • Perform basic risk assessments and use control hierarchy.
  • Participate in drills and maintain records.

Advanced Level

Planning, technical controls, leadership, permit-to-work systems, incident investigation and training others.

  • Design or select engineering controls and review safety systems.
  • Lead incident investigations and root cause analysis.
  • Develop emergency/rescue plans and deliver training.

Example / Case

Workshop workers use nitrile for handling solvents and reinforced boots when moving heavy items.

Checklist

  • Match glove material to chemicals
  • Inspect for damage
  • Use correct boot rating for the task
